Standard First Aid Basics

Look through the topics below in order to learn some safety tips.

Topic Library

Standard first aid is the immediate care provided in medical emergencies, covering everything from minor injuries to life-threatening situations. It includes essential skills like CPR, controlling bleeding, and treating burns, empowering individuals to act confidently until professional help arrives. With basic first aid knowledge, you can make a lifesaving difference in critical moments.

CPR Tips

  • Ensure the scene is safe.
  • Check Responsiveness: Tap the person and shout, "Are you okay?" If they donâ€™t respond, call emergency services immediately or have someone else do it.
  • Position the Person: Carefully place them on their back on a firm, flat surface.
  • Begin Chest Compressions: Kneel beside the person, place the heel of one hand on the center of their chest, and the other hand on top. Keep your elbows straight and push down hard and fast, at least 2 inches deep and at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute.
  • Rescue Breaths (if trained): After 30 compressions, open the airway by tilting the head back slightly and pinch the nose. Give 2 breaths, each lasting about 1 second, ensuring the chest rises between breaths.
  • Repeat cycles of 30 compressions and 2 breaths until emergency help arrives or the person shows signs of life. If you're not trained in rescue breaths, continue with hands only CPR compressions only.

Choking Tips

  • Encourage coughing.
  • Perform back blows.
  • Use abdominal thrusts if necessary.

Wound Care Tips

  • Clean the wound thoroughly.
  • Apply pressure to stop bleeding.
  • Use a sterile bandage to cover the wound.

Burn Treatment Tips

  • Cool the burn with cool (not cold) water.
  • Do not use ice or butter on the burn.
  • Cover the burn with a sterile, non-fluffy dressing.

Fractures and Sprains Tips

  • Immobilize the injured area.
  • Apply ice to reduce swelling.
  • Seek medical attention if necessary.

Shock Management Tips

  • Keep the person lying down and elevate their legs.
  • Keep the person warm and calm.
  • Call for emergency medical help.

Bleeding Control Tips

  • Apply direct pressure to the wound.
  • Elevate the injured area if possible.
  • Use a tourniquet as a last resort.

Heat Related Tips

  • Move the person to a cooler environment.
  • Encourage hydration with water or sports drinks.
  • Apply cool cloths to the skin.

Cold Related Tips

  • Move the person to a warmer environment.
  • Remove wet clothing and wrap the person in warm blankets.
  • Provide warm, non-alcoholic beverages.

Poison and Allergy Tips

  • Identify the substance causing the reaction.
  • Call poison control or emergency services.
  • Administer an epinephrine auto-injector if available.

Seizure Tips

  • Keep the person safe by moving objects away.
  • Do not restrain the person or put anything in their mouth.
  • Time the seizure and seek medical help if it lasts longer than 5 minutes.
